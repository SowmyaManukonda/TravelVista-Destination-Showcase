Amritsar - The Spiritual Capital of Punjab

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Amritsar is from October to March when the weather is cool and pleasant, ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Local Cuisine

  • Amritsari Kulcha: A popular breakfast dish of stuffed bread served with chole.
  • Makki di Roti and Sarson da Saag: A traditional Punjabi dish made with cornbread and mustard greens.
  • Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink, often served sweet or salty.
  • Tandoori Chicken: Spicy grilled chicken cooked in a clay oven.

Festivals and Events

  • Vaisakhi: A harvest festival celebrated with great enthusiasm in April.
  • Guru Nanak Jayanti: The birth anniversary of Guru Nanak, celebrated with prayers and processions.
  • Diwali: The Golden Temple is beautifully illuminated during Diwali, attracting thousands of visitors.

Travel Tips

  • Dress Modestly: Respect local customs, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Carry Cash: Many small shops and vendors do not accept cards.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially during outdoor activities.
  • Be Mindful of Scams: Be cautious of touts and unauthorized guides.

Top Attractions

  • Golden Temple: The holiest shrine of Sikhism, known for its stunning architecture and spiritual ambiance.
  • Jallianwala Bagh: A historic garden and memorial of the 1919 massacre.
  • Wagah Border: Witness the ceremonial border closing ceremony between India and Pakistan.
  • Partition Museum: A museum dedicated to the history of the Partition of India.

Where to Stay

  • Luxury: Hyatt Regency Amritsar, Taj Swarna.
  • Mid-Range: Ramada Amritsar, Golden Tulip Amritsar.
  • Budget: Zostel Amritsar, The Hosteller Amritsar.

Sample Itinerary

Day 1:

  • Morning: Visit the Golden Temple and partake in the langar (community kitchen).
  • Afternoon: Explore Jallianwala Bagh and the Partition Museum.
  • Evening: Stroll through the local markets and enjoy Amritsari cuisine.

Day 2:

  • Morning: Visit the Durgiana Temple and Ram Tirath.
  • Afternoon: Take a trip to the Wagah Border for the evening ceremony.
  • Evening: Return to Amritsar and relax at your hotel.

Day 3:

  • Morning: Visit the Gobindgarh Fort and learn about Punjab's history.
  • Afternoon: Explore the local handicrafts and shops.
  • Evening: Depart from Amritsar.
