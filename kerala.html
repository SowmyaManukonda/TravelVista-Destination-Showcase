Kerala

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kerala is from September to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for exploring backwaters, beaches, and hill stations.

Local Cuisine

  • Appam with Stew: A traditional breakfast dish made with fermented rice batter and coconut milk.
  • Sadya: A grand vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf during festivals.
  • Karimeen Pollichathu: A spicy and flavorful fish dish wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Kerala Parotta with Beef Curry: A flaky, layered bread served with spicy beef curry.

Festivals and Events

  • Onam: A harvest festival celebrated with grand feasts, boat races, and cultural performances.
  • Vishu: The Malayalam New Year, marked by fireworks and traditional rituals.
  • Thrissur Pooram: A vibrant temple festival featuring elephant processions and fireworks.

Travel Tips

  • Carry Light Clothing: Kerala's climate is warm and humid.
  • Book Houseboats in Advance: Houseboats in Alleppey and Kumarakom are in high demand.
  • Respect Local Customs: Dress modestly when visiting temples.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially during outdoor activities.

Nearby Attractions

  • Alleppey Backwaters: A network of canals and lagoons perfect for houseboat cruises.
  • Munnar: A picturesque hill station known for tea plantations.
  • Kochi: A historic port city with colonial architecture and cultural landmarks.

Where to Stay

  • Luxury: Kumarakom Lake Resort, Taj Bekal Resort & Spa.
  • Mid-Range: Coconut Lagoon, Green Gates Hotel.
  • Budget: Zostel Kochi, Vedanta Wake Up Cochin.

Sample Itinerary

Day 1:

  • Morning: Explore Fort Kochi and visit the Chinese Fishing Nets.
  • Afternoon: Take a boat ride in the backwaters of Alleppey.
  • Evening: Enjoy a traditional Kathakali dance performance.

Day 2:

  • Morning: Visit tea plantations in Munnar.
  • Afternoon: Trek to Echo Point and Top Station.
  • Evening: Relax at a hilltop resort.
